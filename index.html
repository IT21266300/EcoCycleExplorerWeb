Literature Survey

Sri Lanka Tourism Research Visual Representation
Research Based

The EcoCycle Explorer project aims to enhance sustainable bicycle tourism in Sri Lanka by leveraging AI-driven technologies. Extensive research highlights the importance of integrating AI in tourism to provide safer, more personalized, and environmentally friendly experiences. For instance, studies by Fernando et al. demonstrate how machine learning algorithms can optimize cycling routes, tailoring them to user preferences and safety requirements.

Recent advancements in mobile and AI technologies have further revolutionized tourism experiences. In the context of Sri Lanka, integrating AI with mobile applications can address critical challenges such as real-time hazard detection, health monitoring, and navigation. The EcoCycle Explorer system aims to be a comprehensive solution for cyclists, enhancing their travel experience by providing dynamic, safe, and culturally immersive routes.

Research Analysis

Research Gap

The EcoCycle Explorer project addresses the following critical research gaps:

Seasonal Route Optimization

Existing systems often fail to consider seasonal variations in route planning. The EcoCycle Explorer system integrates real-time weather data and user preferences to recommend optimal cycling routes, promoting safer and more enjoyable travel experiences across diverse climates and terrains.

Localized Route Recommendations

Traditional travel apps often neglect localized insights. EcoCycle Explorer incorporates AI to provide culturally rich and environmentally sensitive routes. This feature supports the discovery of hidden landmarks and promotes interaction with local businesses, enhancing the overall tourist experience.

Real-Time Hazard Detection

Safety is a major concern for cyclists. Existing technologies lack real-time hazard detection capabilities. The EcoCycle Explorer system employs AI-powered hazard detection to identify road conditions, warn cyclists of potential dangers, and suggest safer alternative routes dynamically.

Location Based Voice Navigation

This study compares the features and objectives of the proposed location-based voice navigation system for bicycle tourists in Sri Lanka with existing systems. While prior studies have advanced navigation technology, they often fail to address the specific needs of bicycle tourists in Sri Lanka.
